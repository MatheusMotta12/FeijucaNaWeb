A História da Feijoada

Feijoada still carries a strong mythology. For a long time it was considered that feijoada would be a dish “invented” in slave quarters. Slaves would cook black beans (originally) from America with the leftover meat that planters despised.

According to Câmara Cascudo, this would be an absurd hypothesis. The Portuguese did not despise these meats. And what the slaves ate was basically beans and flour.

A feijoada (prato com técnica europeia, similar aos vários tipos de cozidos, como os de Portugal, Espanha, França, e outros) teria se desenvolvido no final do século XIX, em restaurantes cariocas. E Câmara Cascudo consagra a origem da feijoada, quando afirma que:

“A complete feijoada is as local as Guanabara Bay.”

Later, feijoada was treated as a symbol of Brazil, bringing together (mythically) Indians, Portuguese and Africans in its origin, consisting of one of several Brazilian myths of racial miscegenation.
